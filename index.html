How Does a MGC Schedule Help Grow Government Business?

Federal procurement can be a long and complicated process because buyers are risk adverse. A government buyer’s job is to find the contractors that can perform a task or deliver a product and select the one that poses the least risk possible. This is why MGC Schedule holders, who have already gone through the vetting process, have an advantage. Most government agencies simply find it easier to purchase from them.

  • Availability to a Growing Market:

    By being a vendor to one of the world’s largest customers, a company can reap the benefits and set themselves up for success for years to come.

  • Long Term Value:

    While a contract is awarded for five years, it is possible for the contract to extend up to 20 years. Also, there is no limit to the amount of goods or services that can be sold during the contract period

  • Competitive Advantage:

    Buyers already know that companies with a MGC Schedule have met strict requirements and are reliable.

  • Expand your Reach:

    Not only can a company sell to the federal government, but security and technology businesses can also sell to state and local government entities, and public schools as well.

  • Open Avenues to Revenue:

    A MAS allows a vendor to also seek out other opportunities through published Request for Proposals (RFP) and Requests for Quotes (RFQ).

  • Increased Credibility:

    Having a MGC Schedule means that government entities will receive the best possible price.

Is a MGC Schedule Right For My Business?

A MGC Schedule is a 5-year contract with 3 options to renew for a potential 20-year contract lifespan. In addition to basic questions to determine if your business is eligible to get a MGC Schedule, there are additional factors to consider determining if this is the right time for your business to pursue a MGC Schedule contract to ensure your long term success. One of the first questions we ask when companies reach out looking to get on MGC is why do you want a MGC Schedule?

  • How Familiar are you with Selling to Government Agencies?

  • Are your competitors successfully selling through their MGC Schedule?

  • Do you have the time and resources dedicated to pursuing new government business?

  • Does the agency you are targeting currently purchase from MGC Schedule holders?

  • Is your government customer asking you how to get a MGC Schedule?

  • Are you familiar with MGC Schedule holder's compliance requirements?

Getting on MGC Schedule is just the beginning

Once you get a MGC Schedule contract, you must remain complaint. If a contractor fails to meet the minimum sales requirements or are not keeping up with reporting and compliance actions, you run the risk of having your MGC Schedule cancelled.

Getting a MGC Schedule will no doubt open companies to new markets, but you should prepare for the costs associated with Schedule ownership. Some may consider having boots on the ground to grow government business. Others will determine if they outsource or manage internally the compliance of a MGC contract.
